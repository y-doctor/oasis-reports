Classification Overview

What is "burial" and how is terminus involvement determined?

Each residue in a protein has a solvent accessible surface area (ASA) — how much of it is exposed to water. When a ligand binds its receptor, some residues get covered up (buried) at the interface. We compute:

Burial = ASA_unbound − ASA_bound

High burial for a residue means it is at the binding interface. We then ask: what fraction of the total buried surface comes from each terminus?

Terminus window: The last/first 10% of the chain length (minimum 3, maximum 10 residues). This scales with protein size — a 30 aa peptide uses 3 residues, a 200 aa protein uses 10.

Classification thresholds:

  • C-terminus: >50% of total burial in C-terminal window, N-terminal window <30%
  • N-terminus: >50% of total burial in N-terminal window, C-terminal window <30%
  • Both termini: Both windows >30% of total burial
  • Neither (structural surface): Both windows <30% — binding is via loops, helices, or other structural features in the middle of the protein

Methods

Three methods were combined. When multiple methods apply, the highest-confidence result takes priority.

1. PDB Structural Analysis — 31 genes (high confidence)

Queried RCSB for ligand-receptor co-crystal/cryo-EM structures. Only verified receptor interfaces were used (partner protein matched against CellTalkDB known receptors). Per-residue ASA burial computed from the RCSB assembly interface data.

2. UniProt PTM Annotations — 39 genes (high confidence)

UniProt "Amidation" keyword indicates the C-terminal amide group is the pharmacophore. The free C-terminus is required for receptor binding.

3. Protein Family Classification — moderate confidence

Assigned based on UniProt protein family (SIMILARITY comment) and Pfam domains, mapped to established structural biology literature:

  • TNF superfamily → C-terminus (type II transmembrane, C-terminal ectodomain IS the ligand)
  • WNT family → Both termini (N-terminal lipid modification + C-terminal finger contact Frizzled CRD)
  • Chemokines → N-terminus (N-term inserts into GPCR transmembrane bundle for activation)
  • Cytokines / Interferons → Neither (4-helix bundle; receptor binds helical faces)
  • FGF family → Neither (beta-trefoil; loops bind receptor)
  • Cystine knot / TGF-beta → Neither (knuckle/finger epitope in middle of fold)
  • Semaphorins → Neither (large N-terminal sema domain, globular)
  • EGF-like → Neither (naturally membrane-anchored at C-terminus)

1. Hit Validation — Receptor Expression Confirms 92% of Hits

Of the 46 hit constructs called across the six comparisons, 41 have annotated cognate receptors in CellTalkDB. For 39 of those 41 (95%), the receptor is expressed in KOLF2.1J at FPKM ≥ 1.0 — confirming the screen is sensitive to ligands whose biology is supported in this cell type. The two exceptions:

  • EFEMP1 — receptor EGFR at FPKM 0.47 (hit at D7 E6 and D14 E6)
  • SEMA6A — receptors PLXNA2 (0.41) and PLXNA4 (0.33) (hit at D7 mTeSR)

Both clear a more permissive 0.3 FPKM expression threshold. The remaining 5 hits (DB133, ERFE, TYPH, C1QT4, ALKB1) have no annotated receptor in CellTalkDB and could not be evaluated.

2. True Negatives Are Shared Across All Six Screens

True negatives — ligand constructs whose annotated cognate receptors are below 1.0 FPKM and which are not called as hits — number 104–105 per condition, with a union of 105 across all six screens and an intersection of 103 (98%). The two-construct difference between union and intersection is precisely EFEMP1 and SEMA6A: they are TN in conditions where they were not hits, but reclassified as hits in the conditions where they reached significance. This near-complete overlap confirms that TN status is determined by receptor expression in the target cell type rather than per-screen variability.

6. Methods

Hit calling (per-sgRNA)

For each of the six MAGeCK sgrna_summary sheets, an sgRNA is called a hit if FDR < 0.05 and |LFC z-score| > 2, where LFC z-score is computed within-sheet as (LFC − mean(LFC)) / std(LFC). Filtered MAGeCK sheets used: Filter_Day{7,9}_{E6,mTeSR}_vs_Day4 and Filtered_Day14_{E6,mTeSR}_vs_Day4. NTC controls (suffix _CONTROL) excluded.

Hit lift-up (gene → construct)

A library construct is "hit" if its UniProt gene prefix has at least one significant sgRNA in any of the six screens. The 30 unique hit gene prefixes lift to 46 library constructs (multi-sgRNA genes contribute multiple constructs to the hit set). This is the library-design view: constructs dropped by upstream MAGeCK QC are still classified by their gene's hit status. Sister-sgRNA agreement is not required.

Receptor expression

Cognate receptors per ligand from CellTalkDB (synthesized ligand pairs only). KOLF2.1J FPKM computed from in-house RNA-seq (mean across replicates). Per-ligand receptor FPKM = max across all annotated receptors. Default expression cutoff: 1.0 FPKM.

Construct-level pool definitions

The 764 non-control constructs partition into three mutually-exclusive pools: expressed (max receptor FPKM ≥ 1), true negative (max receptor FPKM < 1), and no known receptor (no entry in CellTalkDB). Pools are determined by gene-level receptor information and are screen-independent.
